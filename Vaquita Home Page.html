Saving the Vaquita...Before Its Too Late

The Vaquita Porpoise is one of the most endangered animal species with less than 20 individuals left. The biggest danger to its survival is no apex predator, but us humans. They are often caught and drowned in gillnets by illegal fishermen. With populations under 12, it will soon join the other 60% of species humanity has wiped out since 1970. How many more can we stand to lose? If this is not stopped soon, the Vaquita will completely cease to exist

Our Mission

Use artificial intellegence to aid endangered animal tracking in order to conserve endangered species.

About the Vaquita

The vaquita is the smallest cetacean. Its name translates into "small cow" in spanish because of the black patches around its eyes. Vaquitas only live in one small area in the north of the Gulf of California (Sea of Cortez), off Mexico. (not found anywhere else on the earth). They prefer shallow waters along the shoreline, which gets them in situations where they can be caught by illegal fishing nets.

Artifical Intellegence in Cetacean Tracking

Every whale/dolphin fin is as unqiue as a human's fingerprint. Therefore, scientist can use a collection of photos from the coast to coast identify a cetacean's course of travel. This data helps scientist track cetacean populations including the vaquita. Currently volunteers take hours to identify each whale from photos. Using AI to identify each indiviual whale from photos will be more efficent to aid conservation efforts.

How You Can Help

The oceans are constantly being polluted due to our human impact. Taking initiative to save not only these critically endangered whales but overall marine life starts with you! Tow major ways you can take action to preserve their population is by reducing plastic use and donating.

A Big Problem: The plastic that is so prominent in our everyday lives has a very damaging effect on marine life and the overall ocean ecosystem. Each year, 8 million metric tons of plastic are released in our oceans. Thus, cutting down on plastic use is an essential step in cleaning our oceans.

Here are some alternatives to your traditional plastic amenities:

- Bamboo toothbrushes instead of your traditional plastic brushes.

- Reusable or paper straws

- Reusable grocery bags

- Reusable food containers

- Soap bars rather than bottled soap.
